The Minute Between Seconds

6 min read · Fiction

There is a thin slice of time that most people never notice. It exists between intention and action, between breath in and breath out.

For Mara, that slice began to stretch.

It happened first at a crosswalk. The light turned green, yet the city stalled. Cars hummed without moving. A leaf hovered mid-fall. Mara stepped forward, heart pounding, and the world did not follow.

She learned quickly not to panic. Panic wasted the minute. Stillness expanded it.

Each day, the pause returned—sometimes seconds, sometimes longer. Enough time to fix small things. To move a cup from an edge. To read a message she was afraid to answer.

Eventually, she understood: time wasn’t broken. It was listening.

And it had finally given her space to respond.
